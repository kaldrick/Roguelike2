Color Balance

Use the color balance effect to modify the colors of your overall scene render.  Color balancing in photography is most often used to correct differences between the way the human eye interprets color and how a camera captures color.  However; color balancing can be used to produce a wide range of colorization effects, you could turn a day scene into a night scene, create a psychadelic scheme for a dream scene or simply increase the contrast of your rendered scene.

There are six different settings:

Lift:  Color
This will modify the low tones of your scene, shadows or darkened areas.  A white Lift will leave low tones un-modified.  Associated variable:  Lift

Brightness:  Float
This will multiply the Lift setting and is included mainly so you can brighten the Lift value with a brightness value greater than 1.0.  Values lower than 1.0 will give the same effect as moving your chosen Lift color closer to black.  Associated variable:  LiftBright

Gamma:  Color
This will modify the mid tones of your scene.  A white Gamma will leave mid tones un-modified.  Associated variable:  Gamma

Brightness:  Float
The same as the Lift Brightness value, but multiplies Gamma instead.  Associated variable:  GammaBright

Gain:  Color
This will modify the high tones of your scene.  A white Gain will leave high-tones un-modified.  Associated variable:  Gain

Brightness:  Float
The same as the Lift Brightness value, but multiplies Gain instead.  If you're looking for nothing other than increasing the contrast of your scene consider simply raising Gain's brightness value and lowering Lift's brightness value.  Associated variable:  GainBright

Enable or disable Color Balance by modifying the ColorBalanceEnabled boolean variable.
